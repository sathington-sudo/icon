Local Tech Expert Saves Hamilton Seniors Thousands in Ongoing ‘Scam Alert’ Battle

Community Feature

Published: April 2026

While most computer repair shops focus on cracked screens and slow hard drives, one local business has taken on a more vital role: frontline defense against international fraudsters.

Chris, the veteran technician behind Icon Computers on Centennial Parkway, is being recognized by the community for saving local seniors tens of thousands of dollars. Through a combination of technical expertise and a "no-nonsense" approach to customer care, Chris has become a human firewall for some of the city’s most vulnerable residents.

The Anatomy of a Save

The "scam alert" isn't just a notice on his website; it’s a daily reality. According to Chris, the scenarios are often identical: a senior receives a terrifying pop-up warning on their computer claiming their bank account has been compromised or that they are under investigation by the "Cyber Police."

"They come in panicking. They’ve been told not to hang up the phone and that they need to pay thousands of dollars in Bitcoin or gift cards to ‘fix’ the problem."
— A regular customer at Icon Computers

In many cases, Chris has physically intervened as seniors were on the verge of handing over their life savings. By identifying the remote-access software scammers use to hijack computers, he is able to sever the connection, wipe the malicious software, and—most importantly—provide the emotional reassurance that the "threat" was never real.

25 Years of Defense

With over 25 years of bench experience, Chris has seen the evolution of cybercrime firsthand. At Icon Computers, the mission has shifted from simple repairs to advocacy.

"We fix what others won’t," the shop’s motto states, but in Hamilton, that now includes fixing the lives of those targeted by predatory scammers. Local residents on platforms like Reddit have praised the shop as "awesome and honest," noting that Chris often goes above and beyond, sometimes even refusing payment for quick "scam checks" just to ensure a senior is safe.

How to Protect Yourself

As part of his "Scam Alert" initiative, Chris and the team at Icon Computers urge residents to watch for these red flags:

The "Unclosable" Pop-up Scammers use browser scripts to lock your screen. It doesn't mean you have a virus; it means they want you to call the number on the screen.
Urgency and Secrecy If someone tells you not to tell your family or your bank about a "security issue," it is a scam.
Payment in Crypto or Gift Cards No legitimate tech company or government agency will ever ask for payment in Bitcoin or retail gift cards.

For those in the Hamilton area who suspect they’ve been targeted, the advice from the shop is simple: Shut the computer down and bring it to a professional.

As scams become more sophisticated, having a "no-nonsense" expert like Chris in the neighborhood is proving to be Hamilton’s best defense.
